Project Introduction

Project Overview

This project aims to design and manufacture an intelligent unmanned exploration boat approximately 0.5 meters in length for the nearshore and shallow sea area. It focuses on the seabed topography survey in the nearshore shallow waters or the investigation of small-scale marine fishery resources, aiming to address the problems of traditional small-scale exploration equipment, such as poor corrosion resistance in marine environments, insufficient attitude stability, and high manufacturing and maintenance costs. The core solution is: using anti-salt spray 3D printing materials (PETG or ASA) to fabricate the hull shell, optimizing the attitude of the unmanned fast boat in waves through dual-motor differential drive + gyroscope closed-loop control, and integrating shallow sea-adapted radar modules to achieve terrain and fish population detection. The technical challenges include optimizing the hull resistance and designing anti-wave structures, the control accuracy of attitude in dynamic sea conditions, and the anti-interference processing of shallow sea radar signals. Therefore, our team's goal is to manufacture a low-cost, capable of withstanding waves of less than level 3, and having basic shallow sea detection functions, small high-speed vessel.
